Across immer's 180 analyzed functions, the highest structural pressure sits inside src/plugins/arrayMethods.ts, where enableArrayMethods and interceptedMethod are both fire-quadrant hotspots — structurally complex and actively changing today. enableArrayMethods carries a recent activity score of 13.31 and a fan-out of 28, meaning a bug introduced during active development touches nearly every downstream caller in one shot. With 16 critical-band functions in a codebase of 180, roughly one in eleven functions warrants immediate attention.

The table below ranks functions by **activity-weighted risk** — a score that multiplies structural complexity by recent commit frequency. A function that is both hard to understand (high cyclomatic complexity) and actively changing is a higher priority than one that is complex but untouched. CC = cyclomatic complexity (independent execution paths); ND = max nesting depth; FO = fan-out (distinct callees).

## Hotspot Analysis

### `enableArrayMethods` — src/plugins/arrayMethods.ts

Based on its name and file path, this function likely registers or activates immer's interception layer for JavaScript array methods — the entry point that wires up proxied behavior for array mutations. Its fan-out of 28 is the single most alarming metric here: 28 distinct callees means a change to this function can send ripple effects across nearly the entire plugin. It is flagged as both a god_function and exit_heavy, signaling that it handles too many responsibilities and has multiple return paths that each require independent test coverage. Critically, this is a fire-quadrant function with 1 commit in the last 30 days and days_since_changed of 0 — complexity and active development are colliding right now, making this a live regression risk rather than a backlog cleanup item.

**Recommendation:** Before the next commit lands, add characterization tests that cover each of the exit paths to prevent silent regressions. Then pursue extract-method refactoring to decompose the god-function responsibilities into smaller, independently testable units — reducing fan-out by grouping related callees behind narrower interfaces.

### `interceptedMethod` — src/plugins/arrayMethods.ts

This function, co-located with enableArrayMethods in the array plugin, likely represents the runtime interception handler that executes when a proxied array method is called — the hot path for every array mutation immer tracks. Its cyclomatic complexity of 10 means there are 10 independent execution paths to reason about and test, and the complex_branching pattern confirms that branching logic is a meaningful share of its size. With a fan-out of 13 and neighbor_risk flagged alongside enableArrayMethods, changes here frequently require coordinated edits to adjacent code. Like its neighbor, it is fire-quadrant with 1 touch in the last 30 days and days_since_changed of 0 — active development on a CC-10 function is a concrete regression surface.

**Recommendation:** Map the 10 execution paths and ensure each has at least one targeted test before the next modification. Consider extracting distinct branch clusters into named helper functions to bring CC below 5 and make the interception logic self-documenting.

### `enablePatches` — src/plugins/patches.ts

This function almost certainly activates immer's optional JSON-Patch plugin, registering the handlers that generate and apply RFC-6902-style patch objects. Its cyclomatic complexity of 39 and fan-out of 48 make it the most structurally complex production function in this dataset — 48 distinct callees means that a modification here has the broadest blast radius in the codebase. It is a debt-quadrant function: it has not been touched in 43 days and recorded 0 commits in the last 30 days. That dormancy is not reassuring — it means the structural debt has been accumulating unaddressed, and the next developer who needs to extend patch support will inherit all of it at once.

**Recommendation:** Treat this as overdue for refactoring before the next feature development push on the patches plugin. Start by mapping the 48 fan-out targets to identify which callees can be grouped behind stable sub-interfaces, then extract the major operational phases into dedicated functions to bring CC down from 39 toward a maintainable range.

### `handleSimpleOperation` — src/plugins/arrayMethods.ts

This function, also in the array plugin, likely handles a specific category of straightforward array mutations — a support role for the more complex interception layer above it. Its metrics are modest: CC of 3, max nesting depth of 2, and fan-out of 5 place it firmly in the watch quadrant. It has 1 touch in the last 30 days and days_since_changed of 0, so it is active, but its low structural complexity means the risk of introducing a defect is substantially lower than in its neighbors.

**Recommendation:** No refactoring is needed here, but continue monitoring it — if fan-out or CC begins to grow as the array plugin evolves, it is a candidate to absorb complexity that should be extracted out of enableArrayMethods.
